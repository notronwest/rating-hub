Get serves landing deeper
52% deep · needs work • showed up in 3 of 4 games
Your serve depth has dropped to 52% over these four games. When the return team's getting it back at the kitchen line consistently, you lose the serve-side advantage from the first ball. Most short serves landed in the middle third — not faulting, just not pushing back.
Serve deep · 52% Kitchen on serve · 41% 5 flagged moments → 3rd-shot drive trend ↑
Drill: 25 serves with cones at 28 ft. Target 70% deep before next session; expect 2–3 weeks of focused practice to lock in. Pair with follow-through-to-kitchen footwork — they reinforce each other.
2
Close to the kitchen on the serve
41% kitchen on serve · needs work • across all 4 games
After the third shot you're sitting back at 6–7 ft instead of stepping in. The opponent's 4th shot is finding open court because of it. This compounds with the serve-depth issue (#1) — short serve + slow close = easy attack on the 4th.
Kitchen on serve · 41% Player position y=12.0 · target 14.5+ 2 review notes →
Drill: "split-step at the kick" — every time you serve, the moment your paddle releases the ball, take a hop forward. Run it until it's automatic.
3
Choose drop over drive on the 3rd
3rd in · 81% • games 2 + 3 specifically
You drove 9 of 14 third shots in these games — 4 of those got you attacked on the 5th. Drives only work when your team is actually closing up; right now (see #2) that's not happening, so the drop is the safer ball.
3rd drive: 9 · 3rd drop: 5 5th-shot reset rate: 35% 2 sequences →
Drill: coach-fed returns; alternate "drive 5, drop 5, drop 5, drive 1" to retrain the default response. Goal: make drop the unconscious choice when you're out of position.
4
Stay patient in the dink rally
Shot-in 81% • 2 of 4 games
Three rallies ended on a low-margin speed-up that didn't land. The previous shot was actually a great dink — patience would have earned you a better pop-up to attack.
Speed-up loss rate: 60% 3 flagged →
Drill: "no speed-up under shoulder" — drill 3-minute dink rallies where you can only attack a ball above shoulder height. Builds the patience habit.
5
Reset against direct attacks
Direct defense · 67% • game 4
When attacked at your sideline / body, you're countering hard ~70% of the time. Resets back into the kitchen reset the rally and force them to start again. Good ones too — but you only chose reset twice across these games.
